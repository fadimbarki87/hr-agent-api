Types of Questions You Can Ask

1. Employee Lookup

Find employees or employee lists.

Example questions:

  • Show all employees in Engineering.
  • List employees in HR.
  • Who works in the Sales department?
  • Show employees managed by Frank Neumann.
  • List employees in Engineering with their job titles.

2. Employee Details

Retrieve specific information about employees.

Example questions:

  • Show the email and salary of employees in Sales.
  • What is the hire date of Alice Müller?
  • Show first name and email of employees in HR.
  • List employee names and salaries in Engineering.
  • Show job title and hire date of Frank Neumann.

3. Filtering With Conditions

Find employees matching specific criteria.

Example questions:

  • Show employees with salary greater than 80000.
  • List employees hired before 2020.
  • Show employees in Engineering earning more than 70000.
  • List employees in HR hired after 2021.
  • Show employees with salary between 60000 and 90000.

4. Department Questions

Ask questions about departments and budgets.

Example questions:

  • Show department names and budgets.
  • Which departments have budget greater than 200000?
  • What is the budget of the HR department?
  • List departments ordered by budget.
  • Which department has the highest budget?

5. Manager and Reporting Structure

Understand who manages whom.

Example questions:

  • Who reports to Bob Schmidt?
  • Who reports to Frank Neumann?
  • Who is the manager of Greta Wolf?
  • Who is the manager of Hassan Ali?
  • Show employees managed by David Weber.

6. Absence and Leave Tracking

Analyze employee absences.

Example questions:

  • Show all sick absences.
  • List paid vacation absences.
  • Show sick absences with more than 3 days.
  • Show absences for Alice Müller.
  • List absences ordered by start date.

7. Aggregated Information

Ask for totals, averages, and counts.

Example questions:

  • How many employees are in each department?
  • What is the average salary in Engineering?
  • What is the total salary in HR?
  • How many sick absences are there?
  • What is the maximum salary in each department?

8. Ranking and Top Results

Find highest or lowest values.

Example questions:

  • Top 3 highest paid employees.
  • Top 5 lowest paid employees.
  • Show the highest paid employee in HR.
  • Show the most recently hired employees.
  • Which department has the lowest budget?

9. Performance Review Search

Search inside employee performance reviews.

Example questions:

  • Show employees with leadership in their performance review.
  • Show employees with communication skills in their review.
  • List employees with strong analytical skills.
  • Show employees with organizational skills in reviews.
  • Find employees praised for teamwork.

10. Combined Conditions

More complex business questions combining multiple conditions.

Example questions:

  • Show employees in Engineering with salary above 70000 hired before 2021.
  • List employees in Sales earning more than 60000.
  • Show employees in HR with performance review containing communication.
  • Show sick absences for employees in Engineering.
  • List employees in departments with budget above 200000.

11. Semantic Search Over Performance Reviews

Use vector search over unstructured performance review text to find employees by meaning, not only exact keywords.

Example questions:

  • Which employees show leadership potential?
  • Who needs to improve communication?
  • Which employees are strong at mentoring others?
  • Who has strong strategic thinking?
  • Which employees are highly organized?
